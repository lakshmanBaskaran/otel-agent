# Cancellation Patterns

## What it tells you
Cancellations aren't just lost revenue — they're a signal. Where the wash is concentrated reveals policy gaps and demand quality issues.

## How to read it
- Cancellation count vs total bookings → the rate
- Which segments cancel most (OTA usually leads)
- Which months are most exposed
- Is recent cancellation pace accelerating?

## What to look for
- OTA flexible rates → highest cancellation source
- Group wash → groups reduce rooms before arrival
- Long lead time bookings cancel more

## The key principle
State the OTA share as a percentage upfront Always present cancellations with the NET position. "Lost €X but picked up €Y, net is +/-." The GM cares about NET, not just loss.

## Recommendations
- High OTA cancellations → shift toward non-refundable rates
- Group wash → tighter attrition clauses in contracts
- Cluster in one month → overbooking buffer
- Rising trend → check pickup to see net position
