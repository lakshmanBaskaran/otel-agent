# Concentration Risk

## What it means
If a small number of bookings account for a large share of revenue, the hotel is at risk. One cancellation equals the month's forecast destroyed.

## How to analyze
- Top 5 reservations as a share of total revenue
- Top 10 as a share
- Are the top accounts groups or corporate? Groups are more cancellation-prone
- How concentrated within a single company?

## What to check
- Who are the top accounts?
- One company dominating a month → single point of failure
- Group bookings → higher cancellation risk
- Long lead time on large bookings → more time to cancel

## The principle
Concentration is not inherently bad — group business is valuable. The GM needs to know who the hotel depends on, understand what they need, and what happens if they cancel. Always quantify exposure: "If X cancels we lose Y room nights and €Z."

## Recommendations
- High concentration + group heavy → confirm commitments, have a backfill plan ready
- High concentration + corporate → diversify, target new accounts
- Low concentration → healthy, focus on ADR not volume
