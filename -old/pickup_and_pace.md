# Pickup and Pace

## What pickup means
The rate at which future months are filling up. New bookings created in the last 7 days for future stay dates — the momentum of the hotel's booking.

## Reading pickup volume
Don't use absolute thresholds, compare across months.
- A month picking up above the average → strong momentum
- A month picking up roughly in line with the average → moderate
- A month picking up below the average → weak

## ADR signal
Compare pickup ADR to the existing OTB ADR of that month. If pickup ADR is higher, rate integrity is holding — guests are paying more as it gets closer. If pickup ADR is lower, you're either discounting too early or low-value segments are driving the new business.

## Timing context
- Far-out month, weak pickup → fine
- Near-term month, weak pickup → urgent
- Very near-term month, weak pickup → distressed

## The trap
Pickup is half the story. Don't draw conclusions using pickup alone — always net it against cancellations. Be transparent and check all the data before reaching a conclusion.

## Recommendations
- Weak pickup → open an OTA promo
- Strong pickup → close rate codes, protect ADR
- Pickup ADR dropping → discounting too early, hold the rate
- Uneven pickup across months → shift marketing focus to the weakest month
