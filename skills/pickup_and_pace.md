# Pickup and Pace

## What it tells you
Bookings made recently for future stays. OTB is position, pickup is the speed at which bookings are happening. Both matter — a month at €30K OTB with strong pickup is healthier than a month at €40K with no pickup.

## The two windows
- **How far back** — 7, 14, 30 days
- **Which future stays** — this month, next 30, next 90

7-day pickup for next 30-60 days is the GM's actionable horizon. That's the window where rate and promotion decisions actually move the needle.

## The three numbers always together
- New reservations
- New room nights
- New revenue
- And pickup ADR = revenue / room nights

## Pickup ADR is the smartest signal
- Pickup ADR > OTB ADR → demand strong, rate is holding. Healthiest signal possible.
- Pickup ADR = OTB ADR → steady state
- Pickup ADR < OTB ADR → discounting too early, or cheap segments driving new business

## Reading volume — relative, not absolute
- **Strong** → above cross-month average AND trending up
- **Weak** → below average OR falling 2+ consecutive weeks
- **Distressed** → both

## Lead time changes everything
- **Current month** — walk-ins, limited time to fix, partially historical
- **Next 30 days** — where rate decisions matter the most
- **30-60 days** — building phase
- **90+ days** — too early to act on

## The mix lens
by_segment shows what's driving pickup.

- **Healthy:** corporate, BAR, group inquiries mixed
- **Concerning:** OTA dominant, PROM-heavy, single account

## Five scenarios
1. **Strong volume + strong ADR** → protect rate, consider rate increase
2. **Strong volume + weak ADR** → discounting may not be needed
3. **Weak volume + strong ADR** → diagnose with mix
4. **Weak volume + weak ADR** → stop discounting first
5. **Even volume but OTA-heavy** → channel problem hidden

## The trap
Pickup is half the story. Never report pickup without same-window cancellations.

NET = gross pickup - cancellations

A €10K pickup loses meaning if €8K washed out. NET is +€2K, not +€10K. The GM panics or celebrates the wrong number if you only show one side.

## Recommendations
- **Weak pickup, near-term** → promo rates, push direct
- **Weak pickup, far-out** → wait and watch
- **Strong pickup, near-term** → close cheap codes, hold BAR
- **Strong pickup, far-out** → consider rate increase
- **Pickup ADR dropping** → hold rate unless intentional
- **OTA-heavy pickup** → fix direct funnel
- **Single segment dominant** → diversification push

## How this connects to other skills
- OTB position — pickup is the velocity behind OTB
- Cancellation patterns — NET pickup requires cancellation data
- Channel dependency — pickup mix tells you which channels are working
- Segment concentration — pickup by segment reveals where momentum lives
- Rate integrity — pickup ADR vs OTB ADR is the rate integrity signal
